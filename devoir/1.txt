Pop-Up CaféProblem: No convivial space exists on campus during breaks except smoking areas, which are harmful to health.Opportunity: Create a temporary meeting point offering quality coffee at student-friendly prices to encourage student interaction and replace unhealthy habits.2. StakeholdersCustomers (students/professors): High interest, low power.NEOMA (authorization/location): High power, medium interest.Local suppliers (coffee/pastries): Medium interest, low power.3. Value PropositionAffordable coffee (€1.50 espresso).Fast service (<2 min).Social space (bean bags for chatting and relaxing).4. Success Criteria100 sales per day for one week + 80% customer satisfaction (quick survey).5. Rationale (100 words)I chose this project for its social impact: replacing smoking areas with a healthy, welcoming space. It allows me to develop skills in logistics, marketing, and team management while testing a low-risk entrepreneurial concept. The goal is to enhance student life by providing a practical, affordable, and social alternative.6. Major ChallengesNegotiating a central location with NEOMA.Finding high-quality, low-cost suppliers.Retaining customers despite competition from vending machines.Organizing an effective volunteer team.
